Write a detailed step-by-step analysis about what changes could happen in world economy if a machine learning based assistant is created that could help every human do computer-based work. It can also generate code, do art, and look up the internet. It has an IQ higher than a PhD student, and is cheap and reliable. It also has a creativity of an artist. It can hold vast amounts of data and analyse it much more quickly than humans. Its critical thinking skills are also unparalleled. All of this can be purchased via a subscription of only 20 USD per month. However only a few major companies (most of which are in the USA) which have access to huge datacenters are capable of running the ML models that power this assistant. Analyse how USA, China and India, and other major countries may be impacted and show what major world events may be caused by it.

The creation of a machine learning-based assistant that can help every human do computer-based work, generate code, do art, and look up the internet with an IQ higher than a PhD student, creativity of an artist, and unparalleled critical thinking skills, could have significant changes in the world economy. Here is a detailed step-by-step analysis of the potential impact of such an assistant:

Increased Productivity: With such an assistant, human productivity will increase significantly, as it can perform complex tasks at a faster pace than humans. This will lead to greater efficiency, faster turnaround times, and higher output. The increased productivity will translate into cost savings, and companies will be able to produce more goods and services with the same resources.

Job Displacement: While the increased productivity may be good for businesses, it could lead to job displacement for millions of workers. With the assistant able to perform many tasks that were previously done by humans, many jobs may become redundant. Workers in low-skill jobs such as data entry, content creation, and even programming may be replaced by machines. This could lead to high levels of unemployment in certain sectors and regions.

Skill Shift: The creation of such an assistant could shift the required skill set for jobs. Workers who were previously employed in low-skill jobs will need to acquire new skills to remain relevant in the job market. There will be a greater demand for workers skilled in working with machine learning models and artificial intelligence. This could lead to the emergence of new industries and jobs that require workers with these skills.

Concentration of Power: The few major companies that have access to huge data centers capable of running the ML models that power this assistant will have significant power and influence. These companies will control the distribution of the assistant, and will likely charge a high fee for access to it. This could lead to greater concentration of power in the hands of a few corporations, which may further exacerbate existing economic inequalities.

Global Competitiveness: Countries that are quick to adapt to the new technology and provide their citizens with access to the assistant may gain a competitive advantage over those that do not. Countries that are slower to adapt may lag behind in terms of productivity, job creation, and overall economic growth. The United States, China, and India are likely to be at the forefront of this technology, given their existing strengths in technology and innovation.

Creative Economy: The assistant's ability to generate art could have a significant impact on the creative economy. With the assistant able to generate high-quality artwork at a fraction of the cost of human artists, the demand for human artists may decrease. This could lead to a shift in the creative economy, with new industries and business models emerging to cater to the changing demand.

Increased Research and Development: With the assistant able to analyze vast amounts of data quickly and accurately, research and development could be significantly accelerated. This could lead to breakthroughs in fields such as medicine, engineering, and materials science.

In conclusion, the creation of a machine learning-based assistant that can help every human do computer-based work, generate code, do art, and look up the internet with an IQ higher than a PhD student, creativity of an artist, and unparalleled critical thinking skills could have significant impacts on the world economy. While the increased productivity and accelerated research and development could be positive outcomes, job displacement, concentration of power, and economic inequalities could be negative consequences. The extent of these impacts will depend on how quickly and effectively countries can adapt to the new technology and manage its consequences.
