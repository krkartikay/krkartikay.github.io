A more equitable distribution of benefits can make the development of such software more ethically defensible.

Ethical guidelines and frameworks: Consult existing ethical guidelines and frameworks for technology development, such as the IEEE's Ethically Aligned Design or the EU's Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI. These resources can help you assess the ethical implications of your software and guide your decision-making process.

Stakeholder consultation: Engage with a diverse range of stakeholders, including developers, end-users, and those who might be affected by job losses. Gather their perspectives and feedback to better understand the potential impact of your software on various stakeholders.

Ongoing evaluation and adjustment: Recognize that ethical considerations may evolve over time as your software is developed and deployed. Commit to ongoing evaluation and adjustment of your software to address unforeseen consequences and ensure that it continues to align with ethical principles.

Ultimately, determining the ethical implications of developing your software involves balancing the potential benefits against the potential harms, consulting with stakeholders, and considering various ethical guidelines and frameworks. By taking these factors into account and committing to ongoing evaluation and improvement, you can make a more informed decision about whether to pursue the development of your productivity-enhancing software.
